# The Curator Research Series

Welcome to the research series for **The Curator** — a collection of articles, case studies, and analyses exploring the concept of second brains, knowledge management, and the philosophy behind building compounding knowledge systems.

## About This Section

This folder contains peer-reviewed articles and research papers published by The Curator community. Each article explores a specific aspect of second brain architecture, knowledge synthesis, or real-world applications.

## How to Contribute & Comment

We believe in open collaboration. Here's how you can participate:

### **For Comments and Discussion**

Each article has a dedicated [GitHub Discussion thread](https://github.com/talirezun/the-curator/discussions). You can:

- **Ask questions** about the article's concepts
- **Share your own experience** applying these ideas
- **Propose improvements** or corrections
- **Add your own knowledge** and insights
- **Link to related research** or articles

Head to the repo's [Discussions tab](https://github.com/talirezun/the-curator/discussions) and look for the thread matching the article title.

### **For Contributions**

Want to contribute an article or research paper to this series?

1. **Open an Issue** with your article proposal
2. **Share the topic and outline** you'd like to cover
3. We'll discuss scope and fit for the research series
4. Submit your markdown file (or Word doc) for review
5. Publish as part of the research series

See [CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md) for detailed guidelines.

## Philosophy

The articles in this series are grounded in several core principles:

1. **Local-first**: All knowledge systems should be owned by the user, not by platforms
2. **Compounding**: Knowledge should become more valuable over time, not static
3. **Interconnected**: Understanding emerges from connections between ideas, not isolated facts
4. **Practical**: Theory should be paired with working examples and real-world applications
5. **Open**: Knowledge should be shared openly and improved through community contribution
